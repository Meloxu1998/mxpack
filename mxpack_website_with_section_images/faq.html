FAQ for custom printed food packaging boxes

Below are common questions about pricing, artwork checks, dieline templates, China production, urgent shipment options and sustainable packaging practices.

What factors affect my custom packaging price?

As a large-volume manufacturer with scale advantages, MX PACK provides competitive custom printed packaging prices. Price is usually affected by five factors: size, box style, print coverage, material and quantity. Larger quantities usually receive better discounts, especially custom orders of 5,000 pcs or more. If you have questions about price or choices that may affect your order, our support team can help.

How do I know if my artwork is printable?

Our in-house prepress team reviews your custom packaging artwork and checks technical issues such as image resolution, blur, missing bleed, very thin lines and file setup problems. If we find an issue, we mark it on the proof for your reference. If you are not sure how to fix a marked print issue, our prepress representative can help. Please note that our team does not check spelling or grammar errors and does not provide subjective feedback on design content.

Where are your packaging boxes produced?

All of our packaging boxes are produced in China. Our factory combines packaging experience, vertically integrated supply-chain support and modern production management to deliver quality products at competitive costs for global buyers. For urgent orders, we can discuss flexible solutions such as shipping part of the goods by air in advance or accelerating the overall project schedule.

Can you send me a dieline template?

Of course. If you need a dieline for a specific size or box style, please contact us and we will provide it as soon as possible.

What sustainable practices does MX PACK follow?

Sustainability is one of MX PACK's core values. It affects how we source, produce and transport packaging boxes. For materials, we can support recycled and recyclable paper options and work with responsible suppliers. For production, we plan quantities to avoid overproduction and excess inventory, and use precise cutting processes to improve material usage. For operations, optimized box sizing helps reduce material use, filler and shipping emissions.
