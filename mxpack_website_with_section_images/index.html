Frequently asked questions

Below are some frequently asked questions about custom packaging boxes. However, each order is slightly different, so please feel free to contact us if you have any further questions.

What factors affect my price?

As a large-volume manufacturer with scale advantages, we provide competitive pricing for custom printed packaging boxes. Price is usually affected by five factors: size, box style, print coverage, material and quantity. Larger quantities usually receive better discounts, especially custom orders of 5,000 pcs or more. If you have any questions about pricing or choices that may affect your order, our customer support team will be happy to help.

How do I know if my artwork is printable?

Our professional in-house prepress team reviews your custom packaging artwork and checks technical issues such as image resolution, blur, missing bleed, very thin lines and file setup problems. If we find any issue, we mark it on the proof for your reference. If you are not sure how to fix a marked print issue, our prepress representative can help. Please note that our team does not check spelling or grammar errors and does not provide subjective feedback on design content.

Where are your packaging boxes produced?

All of our packaging boxes are produced in China. Our factory combines professional packaging experience, vertically integrated supply-chain support and modern production management to deliver quality products at competitive costs for global buyers. For urgent orders, we can discuss flexible solutions such as shipping part of the goods by air in advance or accelerating the overall project schedule.

Can you send me a dieline template?

Of course. If you need a dieline for a specific size or box style, please contact us and we will provide it as soon as possible.

What sustainable practices does MX PACK follow?

Sustainability is one of MX PACK's core values. It affects how we source, produce and transport packaging boxes. For materials, we can support recycled and recyclable paper options and work with responsible suppliers. For production, we plan quantities to avoid overproduction and excess inventory, and use precise cutting processes to improve material usage. For operations, optimized box sizing helps reduce material use, filler and shipping emissions.
